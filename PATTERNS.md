# Signs of AI Writing — Pattern Catalog

**30 patterns** (as implemented in the leading Claude humanizer skill).

**Primary source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing (WikiProject AI Cleanup)
**Executable form**: https://github.com/egc365/humanizer (fork of blader/humanizer)

This file is the structured reference.

## Content Patterns (1-6)

1. Undue Emphasis on Significance, Legacy, and Broader Trends
2. Undue Emphasis on Notability and Media Coverage
3. Superficial Analyses with -ing Endings
4. Promotional and Advertisement-like Language
5. Vague Attributions and Weasel Words
6. Outline-like "Challenges and Future Prospects" Sections

## Language and Grammar Patterns (7-13)

7. Overused "AI Vocabulary" Words (delve, tapestry, testament, landscape, pivotal, etc.)
8. Avoidance of basic copulatives ("is"/"are" → "serves as", "boasts")
9. Negative Parallelisms and Tailing Negations ("It's not just X, it's Y")
10. Rule of Three Overuse
11. Elegant Variation / Synonym Cycling
12. False Ranges ("from the Big Bang to dark matter")
13. Passive Voice and Subjectless Fragments

## Style Patterns (14-19 + extensions)

14. Em Dashes (and En Dashes) — hard cut in final output
15. Overuse of Boldface
16. Inline-Header Vertical Lists
17. Title Case in Headings
18. Emojis in prose or headings
19. Curly Quotation Marks (in non-typographic contexts)

(Additional later patterns: 25. Hyphenated Word Pair Overuse, 26. Persuasive Authority Tropes, 27. Signposting and Announcements, 28. Fragmented Headers, 29. Diff-Anchored Writing, etc.)

## Communication Patterns (20-22)

20. Collaborative Communication Artifacts ("Great question! I hope this helps!")
21. Knowledge-Cutoff Disclaimers and Speculative Gap-Filling
22. Sycophantic/Servile Tone

## Filler and Hedging (23-25 +)

23. Filler Phrases ("In order to", "Due to the fact that")
24. Excessive Hedging
25. Generic Positive Conclusions ("The future looks bright")
